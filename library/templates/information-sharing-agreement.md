# Information Sharing Agreement (ISA)

> A non-statutory agreement setting out the basis on which two government agencies share information for an agreed purpose.

- **Purpose:** To document the agreed terms under which two agencies share information, including personal information, so that the sharing is lawful, proportionate, secure and well governed. This is a non-statutory operational agreement and does not by itself create new authority to share; the lawful basis must already exist.
- **Owner / completed by:** [PRIVACY OFFICER] (in consultation with [LEGAL ADVISER] and the relevant [BUSINESS OWNER])
- **Related legislation / standards:**
  - Privacy Act 2020 and the Information Privacy Principles: https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-act-2020/
  - Information Privacy Principle 11 (disclosure of personal information): https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-act-2020/privacy-principles/11/

## How to use this template

- Confirm the lawful basis to share before drafting. Identify the specific authority (for example, a permitted disclosure under Information Privacy Principle 11, or another statutory provision) and record it in the agreement.
- Keep the purpose narrow and clearly stated. Share only the minimum information needed for that purpose.
- Specify exactly what information will be shared, how, how often, and how it will be protected at rest and in transit.
- Agree retention, disposal and breach handling up front, and set a review date.
- Have the agreement reviewed by a privacy and a legal adviser before signing.

## Template

### 1. Parties

- **Party A:** [AGENCY A], represented by [ROLE]
- **Party B:** [AGENCY B], represented by [ROLE]

### 2. Purpose

- **Agreed purpose of sharing:** [STATE THE SPECIFIC PURPOSE]
- **Why the sharing is necessary and proportionate:** [BRIEF JUSTIFICATION]

### 3. Legal authority

- **Lawful basis to share:** [CITE THE AUTHORITY, e.g. permitted disclosure under IPP 11]
- **Confirmation:** Each party confirms it is satisfied it has lawful authority to disclose the information described, and to use it for the agreed purpose.

### 4. Information to be shared

| Information item | Contains personal information (Y/N) | Source party | Receiving party | Method of sharing | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Client reference number (example - delete) | Y | Party A | Party B | Secure file transfer | Weekly batch |
| Eligibility status (example - delete) | Y | Party B | Party A | Secure API | On request |
| [ITEM] | [Y/N] | [PARTY] | [PARTY] | [METHOD] | [FREQUENCY] |

### 5. Permitted uses

- The receiving party may use the shared information only for the agreed purpose stated above.
- The shared information must not be used for any other purpose, or disclosed onward, without the prior written agreement of the source party, unless required by law.

### 6. Security and storage

- **Storage location and controls:** [DESCRIBE]
- **Access controls:** Access must be limited to staff who need it for the agreed purpose, on a role basis.
- **Transmission security:** [DESCRIBE ENCRYPTION / SECURE CHANNEL]
- **Classification and handling:** Each party must handle the information in line with its security classification.

### 7. Retention and disposal

- **Retention period:** [STATE PERIOD OR TRIGGER]
- **Disposal action:** The receiving party must dispose of the shared information securely once it is no longer required for the agreed purpose, subject to its own recordkeeping obligations.

### 8. Breach handling

- Each party must notify the other without undue delay if it becomes aware of any actual or suspected breach affecting the shared information.
- The parties must cooperate on containment, assessment and any notification obligations, including any notifiable privacy breach assessment under the Privacy Act 2020.

### 9. Review and termination

- **Review date:** [DATE] (the agreement should be reviewed at least every [PERIOD]).
- **Termination:** Either party may terminate this agreement on [NOTICE PERIOD] written notice. On termination, the parties must agree how shared information already held is to be returned, retained or disposed of.

### 10. Signatures

- **Party A representative (role):** [ROLE]  Signature: ____________  Date: [DATE]
- **Party B representative (role):** [ROLE]  Signature: ____________  Date: [DATE]

## Notes

- An information sharing agreement records and governs sharing; it does not, on its own, create authority to share. The lawful basis must exist independently.
- Where sharing is large scale, ongoing, or involves sensitive information, consider whether a privacy impact assessment or a more formal sharing instrument is needed.
- Keep the signed agreement and review records as part of both agencies' recordkeeping.
- This is a general template to be adapted to your agencies' policies and to specific legal advice; it is not legal advice.

# Information Asset Register

> A central record of the information assets your agency holds, who owns them, and how they must be classified, retained and disposed of.

- **Purpose:** To identify, describe and track all significant information assets so that ownership, value, risk, classification and retention obligations are visible and managed. An information asset register underpins records management, privacy, and security decisions across the agency.
- **Owner / completed by:** [RECORDS MANAGER] (with input from each [BUSINESS OWNER])
- **Related legislation / standards:**
  - Public Records Act 2005 and Archives New Zealand recordkeeping guidance: https://www.archives.govt.nz/manage-information
  - Privacy Act 2020 and the Information Privacy Principles: https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-act-2020/
  - New Zealand Information Security Manual (classification and handling): https://www.nzism.gcsb.govt.nz/

## How to use this template

- Populate one row per information asset. Group related records into a single asset where it makes sense, rather than listing every individual document.
- Assign a clear business owner by role, not by named person, so the register stays current as staff change.
- Record the security classification using your agency's classification scheme, and flag whether the asset contains personal information so privacy obligations are visible.
- Link each asset to the relevant retention authority (your retention and disposal schedule item or an Archives New Zealand disposal authority).
- Review the register at least annually, and whenever a new system or major information holding is introduced.

## Notes

- Keep asset descriptions concise but specific enough that a reader unfamiliar with the area can understand what the asset is and why it matters.
- Where an asset contains personal information, ensure the lawful basis for holding it and the relevant Information Privacy Principles have been considered.
- The retention authority reference should always trace back to an approved schedule or disposal authority; assets with no authority must be flagged for review.
- This is a general template to be adapted to your agency's policies and classification scheme; it is not legal advice.

# Retention and Disposal Schedule

> A schedule that sets out how long classes of records must be kept and what must happen to them at the end of that period.

- **Purpose:** To document, for each class of records the agency creates, the retention trigger, the minimum retention period, and the authorised disposal action (transfer, destroy or retain). A retention and disposal schedule provides the lawful authority and consistent basis for disposing of public records.
- **Owner / completed by:** [RECORDS MANAGER]
- **Related legislation / standards:**
  - Public Records Act 2005: records may only be disposed of with the authority of the Chief Archivist. See Archives New Zealand disposal guidance: https://www.archives.govt.nz/manage-information
  - Privacy Act 2020 (do not keep personal information longer than required): https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-act-2020/

## How to use this template

- List records by class or business function rather than by individual document. Each item should describe a coherent group of records that share the same retention requirement.
- Define the retention trigger precisely (for example, end of financial year, closure of file, last action on the matter). The retention period runs from the trigger.
- State the disposal action clearly: transfer to an approved repository, destroy, or retain (for example, where records are deemed permanent or are subject to ongoing requirements).
- Reference the disposal authority that covers each item. This may be a general disposal authority or an agency-specific disposal authority approved by the Chief Archivist.
- Do not destroy any records under this schedule while a legal hold or active request applies to them.

## Notes

- Retention periods should reflect statutory, regulatory, business and accountability requirements, and the value of the records to the agency and the public.
- Where records contain personal information, the period should be no longer than necessary for the purpose for which the information may lawfully be used.
- Disposal of public records must be carried out under a current disposal authority; an item without a valid authority must not be actioned until one is in place.
- This is a general template to be adapted to your agency's policies and disposal authorities; it is not legal advice.

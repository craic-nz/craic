# Section 23 Transfer Notification

> A notification of an agency's intention to transfer control of public records to an approved repository, under the Public Records Act 2005.

- **Purpose:** To formally notify the Chief Archivist of the agency's intention to transfer control of public records that are no longer in current business use, so that records of long-term value pass into an approved repository in an orderly and authorised way.
- **Owner / completed by:** [RECORDS MANAGER] (signed off by [SENIOR RESPONSIBLE OFFICER])
- **Related legislation / standards:**
  - Public Records Act 2005, section 23 (transfer of control of public records). See Archives New Zealand transfer guidance: https://www.archives.govt.nz/manage-information

## How to use this template

- Use this template once records have reached the point where control should pass to an approved repository, in line with your retention and disposal schedule.
- Confirm the records have been appraised and are covered by a current disposal authority that provides for transfer, before completing the notification.
- Describe the records clearly enough that the receiving repository can identify, accession and manage them.
- Ensure any access restrictions or classifications are stated so the repository can apply appropriate handling.
- Retain a signed copy of the notification as evidence of the authorised transfer.

## Notes

- The transfer of control does not change the public records status of the material; it changes who holds and manages it.
- Confirm format, volume and access conditions with the receiving repository before despatch, and arrange secure transport for any classified or sensitive material.
- Keep the signed notification and any repository acknowledgement as part of the agency's recordkeeping evidence.
- This is a general template to be adapted to your agency's policies and to the requirements of the receiving repository; it is not legal advice.

# Curated Links: NZ Government Information Management, Privacy, and Security

> A directory of authoritative New Zealand sources for information management, privacy, and information security practitioners. Every link below was checked and resolved at the time of writing. Government pages move, so please report any that break.

## Records and information management

- [Archives New Zealand](https://www.archives.govt.nz/) - The national archive and regulator for public sector recordkeeping; sets standards, issues disposal authorities, and stewards records of long-term value.
- [Key obligations under the Public Records Act 2005 (Archives New Zealand)](https://www.archives.govt.nz/manage-information/how-to-manage-your-information/key-obligations-and-the-standard/key-obligations-public-records-act-2005) - Plain-language summary of recordkeeping duties, including the prohibition on unauthorised disposal and the 25-year transfer rule.
- [Disposal of information and records (Archives New Zealand)](https://www.archives.govt.nz/manage-information/how-to-manage-your-information/disposal) - Guidance on disposal decisions, General Disposal Authorities, sentencing, destruction, and transfer.
- [Public Records Act 2005 (legislation.govt.nz)](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2005/0040/latest/whole.html) - Full text of the Act as in force, administered by the Department of Internal Affairs.

## Privacy

- [Office of the Privacy Commissioner](https://www.privacy.org.nz/) - New Zealand's independent privacy regulator; guidance, complaints, case notes, and e-learning.
- [Information privacy principles (Office of the Privacy Commissioner)](https://www.privacy.org.nz/privacy-principles/) - The information privacy principles that govern collection, use, disclosure, and retention of personal information under the Privacy Act 2020.
- [Privacy Act 2020 (legislation.govt.nz)](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2020/0031/latest/whole.html) - Full text of the Act as in force.
- [Information privacy principles, section 22 (legislation.govt.nz)](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2020/0031/latest/LMS23342.html) - The principles as set out in the Act itself.

## Information security

- [National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC)](https://www.ncsc.govt.nz/) - New Zealand's primary cyber security authority, part of the GCSB; advisories, incident response, and security guidance.
- [New Zealand Information Security Manual (NZISM)](https://nzism.gcsb.govt.nz/) - The Government's manual on information assurance and information systems security, the source of technical controls for each classification level.
- [Protective Security Requirements (PSR)](https://www.protectivesecurity.govt.nz/) - The Government's expectations for personnel, physical, and information security, including the classification system.
- [About the Classification System (PSR)](https://www.protectivesecurity.govt.nz/classification-system/overview/) - Overview of how government information is classified, marked, and protected.
- [Endorsements (PSR)](https://www.protectivesecurity.govt.nz/classification-system/overview/endorsements/) - Policy, privacy, and national security endorsement markings such as CABINET, NZEO, and RELEASABLE TO.

## Government digital and data

- [Digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/) - The hub for digital government standards, guidance, and tools, led by the Government Chief Digital Officer (GCDO).
- [Privacy guidance for agencies (NZ Digital government)](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/privacy-security-and-risk/privacy) - All-of-government privacy guidance and tools, and the contact point for the Government Chief Privacy Officer (GCPO).
- [data.govt.nz](https://www.data.govt.nz/) - The government data portal and home of the Government Data Strategy and data ethics guidance.
- [Public Records Act, purposes section 3 (legislation.govt.nz)](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/2005/0040/latest/DLM345536.html) - The statutory purpose underpinning government recordkeeping and accountability.

## AI in government

- [Artificial intelligence (AI) (NZ Digital government)](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence) - Central hub for New Zealand public service AI guidance, led by the GCDO.
- [Responsible AI Guidance for the Public Service: GenAI (NZ Digital government)](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence/responsible-ai-guidance-for-the-public-service-genai) - Guidance on adopting generative AI safely, transparently, and responsibly.
- [Public Service AI Framework (NZ Digital government)](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence/public-service-artificial-intelligence-framework) - The framework setting the direction for responsible AI adoption across the public service.
- [Government Chief Digital Officer's role in AI (NZ Digital government)](https://www.digital.govt.nz/standards-and-guidance/technology-and-architecture/artificial-intelligence/gcdo-role) - How the GCDO leads safe and trustworthy AI adoption.
- [Artificial Intelligence Guidance (data.govt.nz)](https://data.govt.nz/leadership/centre-for-data-ethics-and-innovation/guidance/artificial-intelligence-guidance) - Data ethics, algorithm impact assessment, and responsible AI resources.
